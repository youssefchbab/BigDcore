\section{Introduction}
This chapter describes the architecture of the hardware IDS pipeline, covering its structural organization, protocol parsers, rule checkers, and detection logic.

\section{Design Philosophy}
The IDS is structured as a linear pipeline of independent modules sharing a common byte-stream interface: \texttt{rx\_byte}, \texttt{rx\_valid}, \texttt{rx\_sof}, \texttt{rx\_eof}. Parsers at different protocol layers operate simultaneously on the same data; rule checkers fire one clock cycle after their parser completes. Field extraction and rule evaluation are interleaved with frame reception — no full packet buffering is required.

The byte-stream interface makes each module independently testable and allows the physical reception mechanism (RMII simulation or AXI-Stream DMA) to be swapped without modifying any parser or checker.

\section{Top-Level Structure}
All sub-modules are wired together in \texttt{ids\_top.vhd}. The byte stream is broadcast to every parser simultaneously.

Interface semantics: \texttt{rx\_sof} pulses one cycle at frame start; \texttt{rx\_byte}/\texttt{rx\_valid} delivers one byte per clock; \texttt{rx\_eof} pulses one cycle at frame end.

\section{RMII Receiver (\texttt{rmii\_rx.vhd})}
A three-state FSM (IDLE, PREAMBLE, DATA) assembles four consecutive dibits into one byte via a right-shift register. The preamble state counts at least eight \texttt{01} dibits before accepting the SFD (\texttt{11}). On hardware, this module is replaced by an AXI-Stream receiver without modifying the rest of the pipeline.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Dibit assembly in \texttt{rmii\_rx.vhd}}]
	shift_reg <= rmii_rxd & shift_reg(7 downto 2);
	if dibit_cnt = "11" then
	rx_byte  <= rmii_rxd & shift_reg(7 downto 2);
	rx_valid <= '1';
	end if;
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Ethernet Parser (\texttt{eth\_parser.vhd})}
Extracts destination MAC, source MAC, and EtherType from the first 14 bytes using a sequential FSM (DST\_MAC, SRC\_MAC, ETHERTYPE, PAYLOAD, DONE). On DONE, asserts \texttt{frame\_done} and one of \texttt{is\_ipv4}, \texttt{is\_arp}, or \texttt{is\_unknown}. All outputs default to \texttt{'0'} and are overridden only in the DONE state, preventing latches.

\section{Frame Counter (\texttt{frame\_counter.vhd})}
Accumulates per-second statistics (total, IPv4, ARP, unknown frames) using a hardware divider. A \texttt{one\_sec\_tick} fires when the cycle counter reaches \texttt{CLK\_FREQ\_HZ - 1}, latching accumulators to output registers. \texttt{CLK\_FREQ\_HZ} is a generic: testbenches use 1000 (1\,kHz tick after 1000 cycles); synthesis uses 50\,000\,000.

\section{IPv4 Parser (\texttt{ipv4\_parser.vhd})}
Skips the 14-byte Ethernet header then extracts the following IPv4 fields:

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\caption{IPv4 header fields extracted by \texttt{ipv4\_parser.vhd}}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Field} & \textbf{Byte offset} & \textbf{Width} \\
		\midrule
		Version / IHL            & 0      & 4+4 bits  \\
		Total Length             & 2--3   & 16 bits   \\
		Flags / Fragment Offset  & 6--7   & 3+13 bits \\
		TTL                      & 8      & 8 bits    \\
		Protocol                 & 9      & 8 bits    \\
		Source IP                & 12--15 & 32 bits   \\
		Destination IP           & 16--19 & 32 bits   \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
	\label{tab:ipv4fields}
\end{table}

The IHL field determines the header boundary (IHL$\times$4 bytes), computed dynamically. \texttt{parse\_done} and \texttt{parse\_valid} (asserted only when \texttt{ip\_version = 4}) are driven after the frame ends.

\section{IPv4 Checker (\texttt{ipv4\_checker.vhd})}
Receives parsed fields on \texttt{parse\_done} and evaluates all rules combinatorially in one clock cycle. A local variable \texttt{any\_alert} is OR'd across all conditions to drive \texttt{alert\_any}; individual alert signals allow the scoring engine to weight rules differently.

\section{TCP Parser (\texttt{tcp\_parser.vhd})}
Captures \texttt{ip\_ihl} and \texttt{ip\_protocol} from the IPv4 parser output, then skips to byte $14 + \text{IHL}\times4$ before extracting: source port, destination port, sequence number, and the flags byte (FIN, SYN, RST, PSH, ACK, URG at bits 0--5).

\section{TCP Checker (\texttt{tcp\_checker.vhd})}
Uses VHDL \texttt{alias} declarations for readable flag access:
\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Flag aliases in \texttt{tcp\_checker.vhd}}]
	alias flag_fin : std_logic is tcp_flags(0);
	alias flag_syn : std_logic is tcp_flags(1);
	alias flag_rst : std_logic is tcp_flags(2);
\end{lstlisting}

\section{UDP Parser (\texttt{udp\_parser.vhd})}
Reuses the TCP skip-to-transport logic, activated only when \texttt{ip\_protocol = 0x11}. Extracts the 8-byte UDP header (source port, destination port, length, checksum).

\section{UDP Checker (\texttt{udp\_checker.vhd})}
Evaluates nine rules on extracted UDP fields against a configurable forbidden port set shared with the TCP checker through the rule register bank.

\section{Detection Rules}

\subsection{IPv4 Rules (R1--R12)}

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\caption{IPv4 detection rules R1--R7}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Rule} & \textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Attack / Anomaly} \\
		\midrule
		R1 & \texttt{ip\_version} $\neq$ 4        & Non-IPv4 / malformed      \\
		R2 & \texttt{ip\_ihl} $<$ 5               & Invalid header length     \\
		R3 & \texttt{ip\_length} $<$ 20            & Impossible packet size    \\
		R4 & \texttt{ip\_ttl} $\leq$ 1            & Suspicious TTL            \\
		R5 & \texttt{ip\_frag\_off} $>$ 0         & Fragmentation attack      \\
		R6 & \texttt{ip\_src} = \texttt{0.0.0.0} & Spoofed source            \\
		R7 & \texttt{ip\_src} = \texttt{ip\_dst} & Land attack               \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
	\label{tab:r1r7}
\end{table}

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\caption{IPv4 detection rules R8--R12}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\toprule
		\textbf{Rule} & \textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Attack / Anomaly} \\
		\midrule
		R8  & \texttt{ip\_src[31:24]} = \texttt{0x7F}   & Loopback source (127.x)       \\
		R9  & \texttt{ip\_src[31:16]} = \texttt{0xA9FE} & Link-local (169.254.x)        \\
		R10 & \texttt{ip\_src[31:28]} = \texttt{0xE}    & Multicast source (224--239.x) \\
		R11 & \texttt{ip\_ihl} $>$ 5                    & IP options present            \\

		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
	\label{tab:r8r12}
\end{table}

R8--R10 share a single \texttt{bogon\_check\_en} bit so the RISC-V can disable all three at once on networks where such addresses may legitimately appear.
